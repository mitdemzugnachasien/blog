By Train to Asia

How is that possible?

A short trip from Hamburg to Sylt with Germany's €9 ticket? Or taking the train to London instead of flying? For some, that might already sound like quite an ordeal. To me, it sounds more like a relaxing day trip.

Sylt is 184 kilometers away, London 723. Today's journey takes us 11,783 kilometers toward Asia, until the Pacific Ocean is right in front of us and there's simply nowhere further to go. Over the course of four months, I'll take you along on one of the world's longest train journeys. Starting with the Orient Express, we'll follow the route of the historic Silk Road, eventually crossing China before reaching the rainforests and beaches of Southeast Asia. This blog is about how to plan such a journey, what it costs, and why anyone would choose to do something like this in the first place.
